Caesar, De Bello Gallico 1.1

The following exercise is designed to highlight the steps you need to make when translating Latin texts. When you hover over a word, you can see which words belong together, fulfilling the same function in the sentence. When you click on a word once, a dropdown menu will appear, and you will need to choose between the following: subject, accusative object, dative object, genitive object, ablative, predicative, adverb, prepositional phrase, or verb. Once you double-click on the correct function, the word will be highlighted in colour. When you have gone through a sentence in this way, you will be able to see the function of each word or group of words, and you can start to translate the sentence accordingly. If the sentence has a subordinate clause, you can analyse it in more detail in the line below the next higher clause.
